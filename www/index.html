Children Cancer: still the leading cause of death among children
  • Cancer is the leading cause of death among children
  • Childhood cancer incidence rates have increased by 0.6% per year since 1975
  • Some cancers remain difficult to treat and are in urgent need of new clinical options
  • Current therapies may cause long-term adverse health effects in survivors
Source: ACS Cancer Facts&Figures 2018, p12
Patient Derived Xenograft (PDX) models
  • PDX models retain histopathological and genetic characteristics of original tumors
  • PDXs allow researchers to identify therapeutic agents that work most effectively on tumor
  • Combining PDX genomic profiles and drug responses can lead to less toxic targeted therapies
Who are we
